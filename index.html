Biological Interpretation

🧬

Protein Family

The Serine Protease family (clan PA) is one of the largest and most functionally diverse enzyme families. These proteins catalyze peptide bond hydrolysis via a conserved catalytic triad.

⚗️

Catalytic Triad

The universally conserved His–Asp–Ser triad (positions H57, D102, S195 in chymotrypsin numbering) forms the catalytic machinery. These residues appear as fully conserved (100%) across all five sequences.

🔗

Disulfide Bridges

Conserved cysteine pairs form disulfide bonds critical for structural stability. Six cysteine residues are present in the alignment showing >90% conservation across the family.

🔒

Oxyanion Hole

Glycine residues at positions 193 and 216 form the oxyanion hole, which stabilizes the tetrahedral transition state during catalysis. These are fully conserved across all sequences.

🎯

Substrate Specificity

Variable regions at the S1 binding pocket (positions 189–220) explain different substrate specificities: trypsin cleaves after Arg/Lys, chymotrypsin after large hydrophobic residues.

Evolutionary Relationships

The alignment reveals a clear evolutionary relationship: Trypsin and Chymotrypsin share the highest identity (~78%), followed by Elastase (~65%). Subtilisin (from Bacillus) is the most divergent, reflecting its prokaryotic origin and convergent evolution of the catalytic mechanism — it independently evolved the same His–Asp–Ser triad. Thrombin clusters with trypsin due to their shared specificity for basic residues, despite thrombin's additional regulatory insertions visible as gaps in the alignment.
